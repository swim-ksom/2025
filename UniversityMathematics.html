Introduction to University Mathematics

This series of four lectures provides a comprehensive introduction to foundational mathematical concepts essential for advanced study. We begin by exploring the natural numbers, the principle of mathematical induction as a key proof technique, and the fundamental theory of sets, including operations and cardinality. Building upon the natural numbers, we rigorously construct the integers and rational numbers, examining their arithmetic and algebraic properties, and critically, exposing the inherent "gaps" in the rational number system. Simultaneously, we introduce the concepts of relations and functions as essential tools for describing relationships between mathematical objects, and lay the groundwork for rigorous reasoning by covering the basics of mathematical logic, including logical statements, connectives, and quantifiers. The series culminates with a detailed construction of the real numbers, demonstrating how the gaps in the rationals are formally filled, thus establishing the complete ordered field necessary for calculus and analysis.
