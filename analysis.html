Analysis Course Description

Based on Tao's Analysis - I, this course covers foundational topics starting with the Natural Numbers and Set Theory, including functions, images, and cardinality. It then introduces the Integers and Rationals, Absolute Value, and the concept of gaps in rational numbers. A rigorous construction of the Real Numbers is presented, followed by the Axiom of Completeness and Limits of Sequences. The course explores Continuous Functions on $\textbf{R}$, covering Subsets of the Real Line, the Algebra of Continuous Functions, Limiting Values, properties of Continuous Functions, Monotonic Functions, and Uniform Continuity. Finally, the basics of Differentiation are covered, including definitions, Local Maxima/Minima, properties of Monotone Functions, the Inverse Function Theorem, and L'Hôpital's Rule.
